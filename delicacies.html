Recomended Delicacies

Puto Balanghoy

Puto Balanghoy is a delicacy here in bindoy that street vendors often sell for an affordable price of 5-10 peso. It is a delicious snack that everyone could easily enjoy.

Palagsing

Palagsing is also another version of suman. It is a delicious snack that is very affordable, its price ranges from 5-20 pesos. To achieve the soft consistency of the “palagsing”, it is delicately wrapped in banana leaves and boiled for 30 minutes.

Bukayo

Bukayo is a Filipino dessert made from sweetened coconut strips. It is traditionally made by simmering strips or shredded bits of young, gelatinous coconut in water and sinuklob, which is sugarcane muscovado sugar melted into a chewy caramel-like consistency.

Puto Kutsinta

Puto Kuchinta or kutsinta is a type of steamed rice cake found throughout the Philippines. It is made from a mixture of tapioca or rice flour, brown sugar and lye, enhanced with yellow food coloring or annatto extract, and steamed in small ramekins.

Budbod/Suman

Budbod or Suman is a rice cake originating in the Philippines. It is made from glutinous rice cooked in coconut milk, often wrapped in banana leaves, coconut leaves, or buli or buri palm leaves for steaming. It is usually eaten sprinkled with sugar or laden with latik.

Empanada

The Filipino empanada is a quintessential merienda (mid-day snack) consisting of a flaky crust traditionally filled with ground beef sautéed with onions, peas, raisins, and potatoes. Ironically, empanadas can be found in kitchens all over the world - so many cultures have their own versions.
